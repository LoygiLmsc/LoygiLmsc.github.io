Field School & Self-Directed Study

Title:Can We See What the Numbers Say?
Subtitle: Comparing Visual and Quantitative River Assessments 
Institution: SAIT | Course: ENVS 330 – Environmental Field School (2024)
Description:  ​ During the Environmental Field School, I participated in an intensive, multidisciplinary training program focused on field methods in environmental science. This experience included soil sampling and testing, aquatic invertebrate collection, field safety exercises, GPS-based plant identification, and visual stream assessments. These practical skills were reinforced through daily site visits to riparian zones in the Galatea and Kananaskis River systems.
In addition to the core curriculum, I completed a self-directed research study titled “Can We See What the Numbers Say?”, where I compared the Stream Visual Assessment Protocol (SVAP) with quantitative water quality data. Using methods like Repeated Measures ANOVA and paired t-tests, our team evaluated score reliability, time efficiency, and statistical significance between the two approaches. This work culminated in a scientific research poster and helped sharpen my skills in environmental monitoring, data analysis, and science communication.
